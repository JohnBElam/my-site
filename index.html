About Me

I'm a multi-faceted technologist with a working knowledge of a dozen or so domains: supply chain, telecommunications, manufacturing, product development, operations research, behavioral economics, learning and development, video games and board games — and I like to fancy myself a philosopher.

My career has been a linear progression from engineer to TPM to Product Owner to Product Leader. Colleagues and co-workers describe me as kind, curious, and fierce.

I co-authored The Decision Factory with Adam DeJans (foreword by Warren Powell, Princeton). We co-founded Bit Bros LLC to bring practitioner-first resources to decision intelligence and data careers.

Fuzzy language produces fuzzy thinking. Fuzzy thinking produces fuzzy decisions. I write for people who want to communicate, not just sound smart.
